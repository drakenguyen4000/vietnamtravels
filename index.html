Vietnam Travels

A Nation Rich in Traditions

A land of natural beauty and cultural complexities, of expanding metropolitans and rural ethnic villages, Vietnam is both exotic and familiar.

Sensory Overload

Unforgettable experiences are everywhere in Vietnam. There’s the awe: the limestone island structures of Halong Bay. The ridiculous: the number of motor bikes zooming by in an endless sea of lights and honk of horns as pedestrians manage to cross a street without these bikes even stopping. The inspirational: witnessing the work ethics and entrepreneurship of the people. The comical: mopeds driving down a street with oversized items that barely fit on the back passenger seat. And the contemplative: experiencing the kindness and approachability of the people throughout the country be it in the big cities or in the small villages.

History and Culture

Vietnamese culture is complex, diverse and represents something of a history lesson. The nation's labyrinthine, teeming trading quarters are rich in indigenous crafts and reflect centuries-old mercantile influences. Ancient temples display distinctly Chinese influences in the north and Hindu origins in the south. Meanwhile the broad, tree-lined boulevards and grand state buildings that grace the capital date from the French colonial period. And it's impossible to forget Vietnam's pivotal position close to the epicentre of East Asian power and prosperity, for its cities' skylines are defined by clusters of glass-and-steel corporate HQs and sleek luxury hotels.

A Culinary Superpower

Thailand may contest the top spot, but in Southeast Asia nothing really comes close: Vietnamese food is that good. Incredibly subtle in its flavours and outstanding in its diversity, Vietnamese cooking is a fascinating draw for travellers – myriad street-food tours and cooking schools are testament to this. Geography plays a crucial role, with Chinese flavours influencing the soups of the north, spices sparking up southern cuisine, and herbs and complex techniques typifying the central coastline, rightly renowned as Vietnam’s epicurean hot spot. And up and down the country you can mingle with villagers, sample local dishes and sip rice wine in Vietnam's many regional markets.

Thrills and Chills

If you have the bills, Vietnam has the thrills and chills. Some require a little physical effort, such as motorbiking switchback after switchback up the jaw-dropping Hai Van Pass in central Vietnam. Others require even more sweat: kitesurfing the tropical oceanic waters off Mui Ne or hiking the evergreen hills around Bac Ha or Sapa. And when you’re done with all that adrenaline stuff, there’s plenty of horizontal ‘me’ time to relish. Vietnam has outstanding spas – from marble temples of treatments to simple family-run massage salons with backpacker-friendly rates.

 

Destinations

Hanoi

Hanoi is the capital of Vietnam. Travellers can find well-preserved colonial buildings, ancient pagodas, and unique museums within the city centre. A great place to explore on foot, this French-colonial city is also known for its delectable cuisine, vibrant nightlife, silks and handicrafts.

Ho Chi Minh City

Often referred to as the Pearl of the Orient, Ho Chi Minh City is Vietnam's largest and most populated city. Ho Chi Minh City never sleeps as both night and day vendors work around the clock. There's plenty to do and see in this busy city.

Hue

The ancient city of Hue in central Vietnam was the capital of Vietnam during the Nguyen Dynasty in the 19th Century. One of Hue’s main tourist attractions is the Hue Royal Palace of the Imperial City and recognised today as a World Cultural Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Halong Bay

Halong Bay, "The Bay of Descending Dragons" according to locals, is listed by UNESCO as the 8th natural wonder of the world. The bay consist of towering limestone islands, a rich ecological system, and caves for exploration.

Sapa

Sapa is a small, mountain town in Lao Cai Province. It is located about 350 km northwest of Hanoi, close to the Chinese border. This region of Vietnam is inhabited by different ethnic tribal groups. There are five main ethnic groups in Sapa: the Hmong, Dao, Tay, Giay and Xa Pho.

Phu Quoc

Phu Quoc a beautiful island at the furthest southern region of Vietnam. It's beaches are among Vietnam's best. Get away from the busy city, eat amazing seafood, and enjoy a refreshing coconut as you relax in paradise.
